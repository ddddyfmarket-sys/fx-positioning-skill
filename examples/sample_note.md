# FX Positioning Monitor — 9 Jun 2026
*CFTC TFF Futures+Options Combined · Net = Leveraged Funds + Asset Managers, % of open interest*

**Headline:** USD net long only marginally in aggregate (DXY +5.8% OI) and structurally neutral, but the board is highly dispersed — EUR and JPY shorts sit at their 1-year floors while CHF and NZD shorts have been covered to the top of their ranges.

## Key Takeaways

- **Aggregate USD is close to home but the cohort is split.** DXY net long just +5.8% OI and 48th %ile on full history (Hist Z −0.07), yet leveraged funds run net short USD (−27.2) against asset managers net long (+33.0). USD length pared on the month (MoM −5.2).
- **EUR and JPY sit at the floor of their year.** EUR is at the 1.9th %ile / −2.17z on 52W and JPY at the 1.9th %ile / −1.91z, both cut further this month (EUR MoM −6.7, JPY MoM −11.9). EUR is still net *long* +25.5% OI; JPY net short −34.7.
- **CHF is the mirror case — net short, top of its range.** Net −38.9% OI (the book's largest short) yet 90th %ile / +1.05z on the year as shorts cover; added to again this week (WoW +2.9). On full history still below median (13th %ile, Hist Z −1.09).
- **AUD: structurally large long, tactically gutted.** 5Y 92.7th %ile / +1.67z but only 7.7th %ile / −2.73z on 13W after the biggest weekly cut on the board (WoW −10.4, MoM −25.7).
- **NZD short covered hardest.** +30.7 MoM and to the 100th %ile / +2.07z on 13W — top of its 3-month range — though still net short −36.3 and below its long-run median (Hist 21st %ile).
- **EM carries the largest net longs, almost entirely real money.** BRL +53.2% OI (asset managers +55.5, leveraged funds −2.4) and ZAR +44.9 (asset managers +38.6) — both pared on the month (BRL −15.2, ZAR −12.0).

## Aggregate / USD
The dollar reads neutral at the index level — DXY +5.8% OI, 48th %ile on full history — but that masks a clean cohort divergence: leveraged funds are net short USD (−27.2% OI) while asset managers are net long (+33.0). The 52W window (76.9th %ile, +1.0z) flags USD length near the top of the *past year*, but the 5Y and full-history reads (27th %ile / 48th %ile) put it mid-range structurally. Flow is one-directional: USD length pared WoW −3.0 and MoM −5.2. The story is not the aggregate level — it's the dispersion underneath, with G10 shorts splitting between range floors (EUR, JPY) and covered range tops (CHF, NZD); see `ytd_positioning.png`.

## G10 divergences
**EUR / JPY — pinned at the year's floor.** Both sit at the 1.9th %ile on 52W (EUR −2.17z, JPY −1.91z) and were sold further this month (EUR MoM −6.7, JPY MoM −11.9). They diverge by sign and cohort: EUR is still net *long* +25.5% OI, held entirely by asset managers (+30.3) against leveraged funds marginally short (−4.9); JPY is net short −34.7 with both cohorts short (lev −20.8, AM −13.9). On full history EUR sits above its median (65.6th %ile) while JPY sits well below (13th %ile) — the EUR long is being unwound from a high base, the JPY short extended from a low one.

**CHF — heavily-covered short.** Net −38.9% OI yet at the 90th %ile / +1.05z on the year, added to again this week (WoW +2.9). On `history_positioning.png` it sits in the lower, below-median band (13th %ile, Hist Z −1.09): top of the *past year*, still net short and mid-low on the full range.

**NZD — covered to the 3-month ceiling.** 100th %ile / +2.07z on 13W and +30.7 MoM, the largest monthly reduction in shorts on the board, but still net short −36.3 and 21st %ile on full history.

**AUD — the windows disagree loudest.** Net long +7.6% OI, structurally large (5Y 92.7th %ile, +1.67z) but tactically gutted: 7.7th %ile / −2.73z on 13W after WoW −10.4 / MoM −25.7. Leveraged funds carry the residual long (+11.2) against asset managers short (−3.6). On `momentum_positioning.png` it sits upper-left — long but paring fast.

**GBP — real-money short, fast-money long.** Net −28.3% OI splits cleanly: asset managers short −35.3, leveraged funds long +7.1. Mid-low on every window (17th %ile 52W, 8.5th %ile 5Y) and pared further this month (MoM −10.7).

**CAD — short built hard on the month.** Net −32.8% OI, MoM −27.2 (the largest monthly build into a short), now at the 13W floor (7.7th %ile, −1.44z) though still only mid-range on the year (48th %ile).

## EM
Real money owns the EM longs. **BRL** holds the book's largest net long (+53.2% OI) — almost all asset-manager (+55.5), leveraged funds near flat (−2.4) — but mid-range on the year (40th %ile) and upper-but-not-extreme on full history (75th %ile, Hist Z +0.92); pared MoM −15.2. **ZAR** net long +44.9 (asset managers +38.6, leveraged funds +6.4), 80th %ile on full history (Hist Z +1.0), trimmed MoM −12.0 with a flat week (WoW −0.5). **MXN** net long +40.5, the most balanced cohort split (lev +23.9, AM +16.6), and fairly neutral on range (45th %ile full history, 33rd %ile 52W); flat on the week (WoW +0.6), eased MoM −8.3.
